Summary
Warn users about malicious URLs.
Details
Google SafeBrowsing provides a blacklist of potentially dangerous URLs. MozSecWorld checks user input URLs against that list. In addition, a 3-step verification is used to make sure that there is not a Man-In-The-Middle (MITM) for Google SafeBrowsing:
  1. Checking that a certificate of the Google SafeBrowing url's hostname matches the name on the certificate
  2. Checking that the certificate is not expired.
  3. Checking that the CAs are valid.

You should not blindly trust Google SafeBrowsing and that it has a certificate. To fool people, an MITM attacker can modify the certificate or pretend to be a CA. You should also not trust expired certificate.
