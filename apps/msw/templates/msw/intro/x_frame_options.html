Preventing ClickJacking with X-Frame-Options

Summary Details Diagram Do it Preview Resources Go to Demo!
Category
Cross-Domain Controls
Summary
Instructs the browser to not allow the website from being put into an iframe.
Details
Clickjacking happens when a malicious site loads your web page in an invisible [iframe] and overlays your page with other content, usually a game, or something that entices a user to click in specific areas. The buttons on the game line up to those on the underlying victim web page. When visitors click around on the malicious page (e.g. overlay game), they're unknowingly clicking around your web page (e.g. the invisible iframe). This occurs because the user believes they are clicking on the overlay but the clicks are actually being processed by the framed victim site.

Do It Yourself
In your web server, add the HTTP Response Header: X-Frame-Options: DENY
This will instruct browsers to not render your website within an iframe.

Mozilla's Playdoh automatically sets the "X-Frame-Options" to "deny".
